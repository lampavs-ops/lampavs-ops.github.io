At the 2023 Melon Music Awards, IVE performed a medley of "Baddie," "Kitsch," "Blue Blood," and "I Am."

The performance lasted just over four minutes. It opened with Liz playing a piano introduction live on stage. It ended with the kind of synchronized precision that makes a crowd go quiet before it goes loud.

In the room that night, the judges awarded IVE **Album of the Year**.

It was their first studio album. Released just 16 months after their debut. And it had already been certified million-seller.

That performance was a statement: IVE wasn't just a singles machine. They were artists building something.

## 🎪 The Prom Queens: First Fan Concert Tour (February 2023)

Before the studio album. Before the world tour. Before Lollapalooza.

IVE gave DIVE something more intimate first.

**The Prom Queens** — IVE's first solo fan concert — ran from February to July 2023, visiting Seoul, Yokohama, Kobe, Manila, Taipei, Singapore, and Bangkok across sold-out dates. Total attendance across the full run: **97,000 fans**.

The title "The Prom Queens" was the group's own framing of their identity: not just performers, not just idols, but the ones who arrive at the prom already knowing they're the queens. Confident, present, completely at home in the room.

The concerts introduced songs that fans had never heard performed live — including "Blue Blood" and "Not Your Girl," which debuted during the Seoul shows before the studio album was released. It gave DIVE a preview of where IVE was heading, and it gave IVE a chance to test their live stage before the world was watching.

## 💿 *I've IVE* — The First Studio Album (April 10, 2023)

![First studio album concept atmosphere](/assets/img/posts/ive-part3-album.jpg)
*I've IVE sold 601,669 copies on its first day — the 3rd highest first-day sales by a K-pop girl group in Hanteo history at the time.*

On April 10, 2023, IVE released ***I've IVE*** — their first full-length studio album, just 16 months after debut.

The album had 11 tracks. Seven of them carried songwriting credits from IVE members — a significant creative step for a group at this stage. For a group whose concept centered on self-possession and knowing exactly who you are, having the members' voices in the music wasn't incidental. It was part of the argument.

### "Kitsch" — Pre-Release, March 27

Before the album landed, IVE dropped **"Kitsch"** as a pre-release single.

It went to **#1 on the Circle Digital Chart on the day of release** — achieving a Perfect All-Kill (simultaneously topping all major Korean real-time charts) that year. <cite index="20-1">It was the first Perfect All-Kill of any song that year.</cite>

The sound was deliberately playful — retro-leaning, bassline-driven, colorful — a contrast to the smoother, more formal feel of "Love Dive." IVE showed a different face. DIVE loved it.

### "I Am" — The Album's Title Track

**"I Am"** became one of the defining IVE statements.

Where "Love Dive" was about falling, "I Am" was about standing. The lyrics are a direct declaration of self: *I am my own hero. I decide who I become.* For a group whose name means "I Have," this was the logical destination — a full lyrical and musical articulation of IVE's core identity.

"I Am" achieved a Perfect All-Kill on Korean music charts. The studio performance video surpassed **23 million YouTube views within eight months** — the #1 studio performance video of 2023. <cite index="22-1">"I AM," "Kitsch," and "After LIKE" ranked 3rd, 6th, and 10th respectively on the 2023 Melon Annual Chart — IVE became the first group to have 3 songs in the year-end top 10 in a single calendar year.</cite>

### Album Performance

<cite index="21-1">*I've IVE* achieved the 3rd highest first-day sales of any girl group album in Hanteo history, and became the 3rd girl group album in Hanteo history to surpass 1 million sales in its first week.</cite>

| | |
|:---|:---|
| **Release** | April 10, 2023 |
| **Tracks** | 11 (7 with member songwriting credits) |
| **First-day sales** | 601,669 copies |
| **First-week sales** | 1.1M+ (million-seller) |
| **Year-end award** | Album of the Year — 2023 Melon Music Awards |
| **Label** | Starship / Columbia Records (US) |

### Columbia Records: The US Partnership

Alongside the album's release, <cite index="13-1">Starship Entertainment announced that IVE had signed with Columbia Records for promotion in the United States</cite> — part of a partnership between Columbia and Kakao Entertainment, Starship's parent company.

The signing made IVE one of only a small number of K-pop acts with a major US label partnership, opening doors to American market promotion that most groups access only through independent channels.

## 🎧 Editor's Pick — "I Am"

> *A personal note from the Editor.*

"After Like" is the song that pulled me into IVE. But **"I Am"** is the one I keep coming back to — especially on the hard days.

The melody is beautiful on its own terms. But it's the lyrics that hit differently.

The song opens by telling you to open a different door — that you don't need to follow anyone else's path. *You go your way, I'll go mine.* Simple words. But delivered with that level of conviction, at that tempo, with that arrangement underneath — it doesn't feel like a pop lyric. It feels like someone saying something true.

And then the song builds. And builds. And at the climax, it releases everything at once — the image of being completely lost on a deep dark night, and the answer arriving not as a map or directions, but as permission: *just fly up. Wherever you pass, that becomes the road.* One, two, three. Fly up.

That is not a self-help slogan. That is a philosophy. And when the six of them deliver it together at full volume, something in you lifts.

I've listened to K-pop for a while now. I've heard a lot of songs about confidence and self-belief — it's a crowded genre. Most of them feel like encouragement from the outside. **"I Am" feels like it's coming from inside you.** Like a voice that was already there, just waiting to be loud enough to hear.

There have been moments — genuinely difficult moments, the kind where the next step looks impossible — where I put this song on. Not consciously, not as a choice. It just came to me. And something in the combination of that melody, those words, and the way IVE performs them made the next step feel possible again.

That's not something I say about many songs.

If you only ever listen to one IVE song, make it "After Like" — it will make you a fan. But if you want to understand what IVE is actually *saying*, make it "I Am." It might stay with you longer than you expect.

## 🌏 *I've Mine* and "Baddie" — EP #1 (October 2023)

In October 2023, IVE released their **first Korean-language EP**, ***I've Mine***.

The lead single **"Baddie"** was IVE at their most assertive — a harder edge, more confident posture, a sound that leaned into hip-hop influences without abandoning the pop precision that defined their earlier work. It debuted at **#1 on the Circle Digital Chart** and achieved a Perfect All-Kill — IVE's fourth #1 single in Korea.

*I've Mine* first-week sales: **1.6 million copies** — the third highest first-week sales ever for a K-pop girl group at the time. <cite index="22-1">The distributor surpassed 2 million copies as of March 2024.</cite>

## 🌍 Show What I Have: The First World Tour (October 2023 — September 2024)

In October 2023, IVE launched their **first world tour** — *Show What I Have*.

The tour ran for nearly a full year, across **37 concerts in 26 cities across 18 countries**, covering Asia, North America, Europe, Latin America, and Oceania.

| Region | Cities |
|:---|:---|
| Asia | Seoul, Yokohama, Fukuoka, Osaka, Kuala Lumpur, Singapore, Taipei, Hong Kong, Manila, Tokyo |
| North America | Inglewood (Kia Forum), Newark (Prudential Center) |
| Europe | London (The O2), Paris, Berlin |
| Latin America | Mexico City, São Paulo |
| Oceania | Melbourne |

### Tokyo Dome — A Historic Finale

The world tour concluded at **Tokyo Dome** on September 4–5, 2024. All 95,800 tickets for both days sold out in **10 seconds** when reservations opened.

<cite index="22-1">This made IVE the 6th K-pop girl group ever to perform at Tokyo Dome, and the first Starship Entertainment artist to do so in the label's 16-year history.</cite>

### What the Critics Said

The Daily Star's Jack Wetherill, reviewing the London O2 show, wrote that "I've been to countless concerts in my 31 years, but I can safely say IVE at The O2 was the best I've ever seen." Billboard's Jeff Benjamin described the Newark show as proof that "IVE has already carved out a distinct identity and color as the prom queens of K-pop."

DIVE, who followed the tour across multiple cities on multiple continents, agreed.

## 🎛️ *IVE Switch* and "Heya" — EP #2 (April 2024)

On April 29, 2024, IVE released ***IVE Switch*** — their second EP — with two lead singles that went in completely opposite directions.

**"Heya" (해야)** was inspired by a Korean folk tale the members invented themselves: a tiger who loved the sun. The music video leaned into Korean traditional aesthetics, vibrant and warm. The song peaked in the **top 10 on the Circle Digital Chart** and became a live concert staple for the rest of the year.

**"Accendio"** took the opposite path: dark, synth-heavy, spell-like, with lyrics about magic. Wonyoung said it reminded her of "Love Dive" but "more developed" — a darker evolution of the same confident energy.

The contrast between the two singles showed DIVE something important: IVE wasn't locked into one sound. They could move between warmth and danger, tradition and futurism, without losing coherence.

## 🎸 Lollapalooza Chicago 2024 — The Western Validation

On **August 3, 2024**, IVE performed at **Lollapalooza** in Chicago — one of the biggest music festivals in the United States.

They performed **11 songs with full choreography** on the main stage. For a K-pop girl group, that level of performance — in front of a Western festival audience that hadn't necessarily come specifically for K-pop — was a test. IVE passed it.

The performance generated strong coverage in US music media and confirmed what their Columbia Records partnership had signaled: IVE was being positioned for American market growth, not just promoted there.

In 2025, they would return to Lollapalooza — this time in Berlin and then Paris, becoming <cite index="16-1">the first K-pop girl group to perform at Lollapalooza in two consecutive years across three different cities.</cite>

## 🤝 David Guetta Collab — "Supernova Love" (November 2024)

On November 8, 2024, IVE released **"Supernova Love"** — a collaboration with French DJ and record producer **David Guetta**.

The partnership was significant for what it represented: one of the world's biggest EDM producers choosing to work with a K-pop girl group, not because K-pop was trendy, but because IVE's sound was compatible with a Western dance music framework.

"Supernova Love" expanded IVE's reach into European and American dance music audiences — listeners who might never have encountered a K-pop act through Korean chart channels, but who found IVE through Guetta's established fanbase.

## 🔑 What the Artist Era Meant

Between 2023 and 2024, IVE made a transition that many K-pop groups struggle with: from a group defined by its singles to a group with a coherent artistic identity.

The studio album gave them depth. The world tour gave them presence. The Columbia Records partnership gave them infrastructure. The Lollapalooza performance gave them Western credibility. And the member songwriting credits gave them ownership.

IVE didn't just grow in this period. They defined what "grown-up IVE" was going to look like. By the end of 2024, they had answered the question their debut raised: *what happens after the perfect debut?*

The answer was: *this.*
