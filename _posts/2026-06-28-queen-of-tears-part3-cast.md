## ✍️ Writer — Park Ji-eun (박지은)

> 🎬 [**▶ Watch Queen of Tears behind-the-scenes on YouTube**](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Queen+of+Tears+behind+the+scenes+making+Park+Ji+eun)

### Two Consecutive tvN Records

**Crash Landing on You** (2019–2020) — set tvN's all-time rating record at the time: 21.7%

**Queen of Tears** (2024) — broke that same record: 24.850%

Park Ji-eun wrote both of tvN's two most recent all-time highest-rated dramas back to back — the writer beat her own record.

Both shows share something. A love story in an impossible situation. Two people from different worlds. Walls that seem impossible to break. And two people who refuse to give up.

### How She Writes Love

Park Ji-eun's dramas tend to place romance behind unusually high barriers — national division, class divides, illness, and family conflict among them. The pattern holds across her filmography: love that has to survive something the characters cannot control.

In Queen of Tears, the obstacles are a terminal illness, memory loss, and a family conspiracy decades in the making.

## 🎬 The Directors — Kim Hee-won & Jang Young-woo

**Director Kim Hee-won** made *Vincenzo* (2021) and *Little Women* (2022) before Queen of Tears — moving from a crime-comedy thriller to a wealth-and-betrayal drama to a romantic melodrama. She is known for a visually striking style — natural lighting, careful composition — that earned her the nickname "God Hee-won" among Korean viewers, and a Best Director nomination at the 57th Baeksang Arts Awards for *Vincenzo*.

**Director Jang Young-woo** is known for *Crash Landing on You* and the *Sweet Home* / *Bulgasal* line of dark-fantasy work. That same eye for atmosphere and emotional tension carried over into Queen of Tears.

Together, the two directors won **Best Direction (Fiction)** at the 2024 Asian Academy Creative Awards for their work on the series.

| Director | Known for |
|----------|-----------|
| Kim Hee-won | Vincenzo (2021) · Little Women (2022) · Queen of Tears (2024) |
| Jang Young-woo | Crash Landing on You (2019–2020) · Sweet Home / Bulgasal series · Queen of Tears (2024) |

> 📸 **Official SNS:** [Instagram @soohyun_k216](https://www.instagram.com/soohyun_k216)

> 🎬 [**▶ Watch Kim Soo-hyun's best Queen of Tears moments on YouTube**](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Kim+Soo+hyun+Queen+of+Tears+best+scenes)

### Who Is He?

Kim Soo-hyun is one of the biggest names in Korean drama — and has been for over a decade.

Queen of Tears marked Kim Soo-hyun's return to a leading TV drama role roughly three years after *One Ordinary Day* (2021) — not his first project back after military service, which was *It's Okay to Not Be Okay* (2020). Even so, ahead of the show's airing, media reports speculated about an exceptionally high per-episode fee; the production denied the widely circulated figure, and the actual contract terms were never publicly disclosed.

The role of Hyun-woo is technically demanding. He starts as a man ready to walk away. He becomes a man who will not leave no matter what. And in between, he has to hide who he is from someone who has forgotten him. All of that lives in Kim Soo-hyun's face — without a speech, without explanation.

### Kim Ji-won (김지원) — Hong Hae-in

> 📸 **Official SNS:** [Instagram @k_jiwon9](https://www.instagram.com/k_jiwon9)

> 🎬 [**▶ Watch Kim Ji-won's best Queen of Tears moments on YouTube**](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Kim+Ji+won+Queen+of+Tears+best+scenes)

### Who Is She?

Kim Ji-won had already built a strong reputation as a leading actress well before Queen of Tears. *Fight for My Way* (2017), *Arthdal Chronicles* (2019), *Lovestruck in the City* (2020), and *My Liberation Notes* (2022) had all put her in central roles, each earning her praise for restrained, precise emotional acting.

Queen of Tears took that recognition to a much larger, more global audience — and the role became her most widely seen work internationally.

Hae-in is a character who communicates through silence. She does not have long speeches. She has micro-expressions — a slight shift in the eyes, a jaw that barely tightens. Kim Ji-won delivered all of it, and the performance became one of the show's most discussed elements, though the Baeksang Best Actress award that year went to Lee Ha-nee for *Knight Flower* — Kim Ji-won was not among that category's nominees.

### Kwak Dong-yeon (곽동연) — Hong Soo-cheol

> 📸 **Official SNS:** [Instagram @dy.__.yeon](https://www.instagram.com/dy.__.yeon)

### Who Is He?

The biggest surprise of the whole show.

Hong Soo-cheol is Hae-in's younger brother. He tries very hard to be powerful and important. He fails, constantly, in the most entertaining ways possible. Every scene he is in becomes funnier than it should be.

Kwak Dong-yeon was already a respected actor from Vincenzo and Big Mouth. His performance in Queen of Tears was widely praised in reviews and fan discussion as a standout comic presence — the kind of supporting role that ends up defining a scene-stealer.

### Park Sung-hoon (박성훈) — Yoon Eun-sung

> 📸 **Official SNS:** [Instagram @parks_hunnn](https://www.instagram.com/parks_hunnn)

### Who Is He?

Yoon Eun-sung is the show's main source of tension. A former Wall Street analyst. Obsessively connected to Hae-in's past. He returns to Korea with a plan.

Park Sung-hoon drew widespread attention for his intensity playing a bully in The Glory. He brings a similarly sharp edge to Yoon Eun-sung — but this character is more complicated. The show gradually reveals the abandonment and manipulation that shaped him, complicating any simple read of him as pure villain.

## 🎭 Why the "Two Kims" Chemistry Worked

The biggest reason Queen of Tears succeeded is the chemistry between Kim Soo-hyun and Kim Ji-won.

This was actually their **first time appearing together on screen**. Kim Soo-hyun had previously worked with writer Park Ji-eun on *My Love from the Star* — but that show starred Jun Ji-hyun, not Kim Ji-won. The pairing generated significant anticipation not because of a reunion, but because both actors already had strong individual reputations in major romance dramas, and audiences were curious how two performers known for very different acting styles would play off each other.

The moment they appeared on screen together, audiences felt it. Some critics and fans described the connection as immediate — the kind of on-screen chemistry that's difficult to fully attribute to writing alone.
