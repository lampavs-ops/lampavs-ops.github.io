In 2022, IVE released three songs.

All three went to #1 in South Korea.

One of them — "Love Dive" — was named Song of the Year at four major award ceremonies simultaneously. It wasn't even their debut song. They had only been a group for one year.

At the end of that year, IVE stood at a podium at the Melon Music Awards and accepted the Daesang — the grand prize, the highest honor in Korean music. They had been a group for exactly 360 days.

It was the third fastest a girl group had ever won a Daesang after debut. And they weren't done.

## 📍 First: "Eleven" Finishes What It Started (Early 2022)

IVE's debut song "Eleven" had launched in December 2021 — but it kept winning through early 2022.

By the time "Eleven" was done collecting trophies, it had accumulated **13 music show wins** — an extraordinary number for any debut song. The song became a cultural marker: the IVE dance challenge spread across TikTok globally, and the phrase "eleven" temporarily became shorthand in Korean fan communities for "a perfect debut."

At the year-end award shows spanning 2021 into early 2022, IVE swept every major **Rookie of the Year** award:

| Ceremony | Award |
|:---|:---|
| Melon Music Awards | New Artist of the Year |
| MAMA Awards | Best New Female Artist |
| Golden Disc Awards | Rookie Artist of the Year |
| Hanteo Music Awards | New Artist of the Year |
| Asia Artist Awards | New Artist of the Year |
| Genie Music Awards | New Artist of the Year |

Every single one. Clean sweep. For a group that had debuted five weeks before the end of 2021, this was remarkable. But rookies sweep rookie awards. What came next was the part that nobody saw coming.

## 🌊 "Love Dive" — The Song That Rewrote the Year (April 5, 2022)

![After Like disco stage performance atmosphere](/assets/img/posts/ive-part2-afterlike.jpg)
*"Love Dive" stayed at #1 on Korea's year-end chart for all of 2022 — released in April, it defined the entire year.*

On April 5, 2022, IVE released their second single album, ***Love Dive***.

The title track debuted at **#1 on the Circle Digital Chart** — IVE's first Korean chart-topper. Then it stayed. For four consecutive weeks at #1. For 29 consecutive weeks in the top 10. For the entire rest of the year in the cultural conversation.

At the end of 2022, when Melon and Circle published their annual charts, **"Love Dive" ranked #1 for the entire year** — the single most-streamed and most-downloaded song in South Korea across all of 2022.

Not the #1 K-pop song. The #1 song. Period.

### The Secret Origin of "Love Dive"

There's a detail that became famous: **Sunmi was offered "Love Dive" first.**

In a later radio interview, veteran idol Sunmi revealed that she had been given the song before IVE and turned it down — feeling it would be "too cliché" for her style, and that a girl group would suit it better. She praised IVE for how they delivered it.

That origin story said something important: "Love Dive" was a song waiting for the right group. IVE was that group.

### The Daesang Sweep

"Love Dive" didn't just top the charts. It won Song of the Year at **every major award ceremony** at the end of 2022:

| Ceremony | Award |
|:---|:---|
| 🏆 Melon Music Awards | Song of the Year (Daesang) |
| 🏆 MAMA Awards | Song of the Year (Daesang) |
| 🏆 Golden Disc Awards | Song of the Year (Daesang) |
| 🏆 Asia Artist Awards | Song of the Year (Daesang) |

Four Daesangs. With a debut-year song. From a group that had existed for less than 12 months.

<cite index="9-1">Snagging the award just 360 days since their debut, IVE's achievement made them the third fastest girl group to win their first grand prize, overtaking aespa's record of 380 days in 2021.</cite>

In Korean music industry terms, this was not a milestone. It was a statement.

### What Made "Love Dive" Work

The song operates on a deceptively simple idea: falling in love as an act of diving — the suspended moment before impact, simultaneously terrifying and thrilling. The choreography mirrors this: fluid, controlled, with moments of deliberate suspension that match the song's emotional logic.

The chorus was immediately earwormy in a way that spread faster through social media than almost anything else released that year. The "dive" hand motion became one of K-pop's most recreated challenge dances.

But underneath the pop accessibility, the song had craft. The production resisted the biggest, loudest choices at every turn — choosing restraint where other tracks would have pushed. That restraint is what made it last.

## ⚡ "After Like" — The Third Consecutive #1 (August 22, 2022)

Four months after "Love Dive," IVE came back.

**"After Like"** sampled Gloria Gaynor's disco classic "I Will Survive" — a bold creative decision that could easily have felt gimmicky. Instead, it felt earned. The disco DNA gave "After Like" an immediate warmth and familiarity that made it accessible without being predictable.

It debuted at **#1 on the Circle Digital Chart**. IVE's third single. Third straight chart-topper.

"After Like" also collected **10 music show wins** — bringing IVE's 2022 total to more than 30 wins across just three singles. In K-pop, music show wins are the week-by-week currency of chart performance. 30+ wins in one year, across three songs, with no filler — that's not a good year. That's a historic one.

<cite index="9-1">"After Like" ranked number one on the Circle year-end chart for 2022 alongside "Love Dive," making IVE the first girl group to place two songs simultaneously in the Circle year-end top 3.</cite>

## 🎧 Editor's Top Pick — "After Like"

> *A personal note from the Editor.*

I first heard "After Like" completely by accident — it came up in a playlist I wasn't paying close attention to.

Then I was paying very close attention.

What got me wasn't any single element. It was the combination. The melody keeps shifting — it doesn't settle into one groove and stay there, it keeps giving you something new every eight bars, layering in textures that reward repeated listening. Underneath that, the members' voices blend in a way that feels genuinely musical rather than just technically precise — you can hear individual character in each vocal line, and somehow it all coheres into something bigger than the sum of its parts. And then there's the choreography: sharp angles softened by the disco warmth of the production, six people moving as one organism that still somehow reads as six completely different personalities.

Melody. Voice. Dance. All three working at full capacity, all three in the same four minutes.

I ended up on repeat for days. Not "one more time before moving on" — actual days, finding something new on each listen, genuinely not getting tired of it.

**And then I noticed An Yujin.**

She had been in my peripheral vision throughout — she's hard to miss, she's IVE's main dancer and one of the most instinctively expressive performers in the group. But watching "After Like" stage footage closely, I kept coming back to her. The way she performs isn't just technically correct — it's inhabited. She's *in* the song, not just executing it. There's a directness to her stage presence that makes you feel like she's performing specifically for you, which is the rarest quality any performer can have.

After "After Like," I went deep into the Yujin rabbit hole. Stage footage, interviews, variety show clips. She held up in every format. The wit in interviews. The warmth in fan interactions. The complete transformation into a different mode of focus the moment the performance starts.

If you're new to IVE and looking for a place to start — **this song, this performance, this member.** You'll understand the rest from there.

## 🎧 Editor's Top Pick — "I Am"

> *Another personal note from the Editor.*

If "After Like" was the song that pulled me in, **"I Am"** is the one that made me stay.

I know — "I Am" is technically a 2023 release, from IVE's first studio album. But I discovered it in the same late-night listening session that started with "After Like," and in my head the two songs live together as a pair. Two sides of the same IVE: one that makes you move, one that makes you feel.

What "I Am" does differently is take that same IVE confidence — *I have everything, I know who I am* — and makes it feel earned rather than announced. The production is more restrained than "After Like." There's space in it. The melody doesn't chase you — it waits for you to come to it. And when the chorus opens up, the payoff is enormous precisely because the song took its time getting there.

Lyrically it's simple on the surface: *I am the hero of my own story. I decide who I become.* But the way the members deliver it — especially in live performance — it doesn't feel like a slogan. It feels like something they actually believe. There's a sincerity in the vocal performance that the production supports rather than overwhelms.

I've watched the "I Am" studio performance video more times than I'll admit here. The choreography has a kind of controlled stillness that contrasts with the energy underneath the music — moments where six people simply stand in formation and *own* the frame before exploding into movement again. It's sophisticated staging for a group in their second year.

These two songs — "After Like" and "I Am" — are my personal recommendation if you're deciding whether IVE is worth your time.

Start with "After Like." Let it run for a few days. Then move to "I Am."

By the end of that week, you'll understand why IVE is different.

## 🏆 Year-End Awards: The Full Picture

The 2022 year-end ceremony season was IVE's formal coronation. Beyond the four Song of the Year Daesangs for "Love Dive," they also took:

- **Best Female Group** — Melon Music Awards, MAMA Awards
- **New Artist of the Year** — Melon Music Awards (alongside NewJeans)
- **Best Dance Performance Female Group** — MAMA Awards
- **Top 10 Artists** — Melon, Circle, Genie, and multiple charts
- **Artist of the Year – Global Digital Music** — Circle Chart Music Awards (for both "Love Dive" and their debut period)

<cite index="8-1">The success of their debut led IVE to win Rookie of the Year awards at the 7th Asia Artist Awards, 2022 Genie Music Awards, 37th Golden Disc Awards, 2021 Hanteo Music Awards, 2022 Melon Music Awards, and 2022 MAMA Awards.</cite>

They won rookie awards and grand prizes **in the same year**. That combination — the 4th time any group had achieved it in K-pop history — was the headline of their debut year.

## 🌍 DIVE: The Fandom Takes Shape

Throughout 2022, DIVE wasn't just growing — it was organizing.

IVE's fanbase developed some characteristics that distinguished it from typical 4th-gen fandoms:

**① Cross-generational reach.** IVE's music appealed to listeners in their teens and their thirties simultaneously. The confidence of the concept, combined with the accessibility of the melodies, made them unusually broad in reach.

**② Southeast Asian depth.** Rei's Japanese background and the group's overall global positioning created early traction in Japan, Thailand, Vietnam, and across Southeast Asia.

**③ Streaming discipline.** DIVE developed a reputation for organized streaming efforts that consistently pushed IVE's songs to the top of daily charts in the weeks following release — a skill that amplified what was already strong music.

**④ Wonyoung's brand spillover.** Wonyoung's individual visibility — as a music bank MC, as a brand ambassador, as a fashion presence — consistently drove new listeners toward IVE's music. Her individual fanbase and the group's fanbase fed each other in ways that multiplied both.

## 💡 Why 2022 Was So Important

It would have been enough to be a great rookie group in 2022. IVE wasn't content with that.

What made 2022 historically significant for IVE was that they didn't peak at debut and then settle. Each release in 2022 was *bigger* than the last:

- "Eleven" proved they could debut cleanly and win immediately.
- "Love Dive" proved they could sustain and define a year.
- "After Like" proved they could follow a phenomenon with another phenomenon.

That upward trajectory — each release escalating, not just maintaining — is the rarest quality in K-pop. It's what separates groups that have a good debut year from groups that build empires.

By December 2022, IVE had done something almost no group in the 4th generation had managed: they had made everyone else ask "what are they going to do next?" instead of "can they keep it going?"

That question had a very good answer.
